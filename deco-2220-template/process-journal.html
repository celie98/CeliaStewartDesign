Process Journal

Introduction

The design brief is to solve a design problem through using the user-centred design process including analysis, synthesis and user evaluation. The aim is to develop a usable screen-based visual interface for the future to assist people to interact with a useful autonomous mobile or drone involving the chosen problem scenario or context. With using practical skills in designing and prototyping interactive products/ services and working as a team of three negotiating, researching and bouncing ideas off each other to develop a sound user experience and user interface for people in the future to interact with autonomous vehicles and drones.

WEEK 2

The following week we developed a deeper understanding of our design brief and what are autonomous mobiles and drones. We talked about what they will do and how they will change society in the future. Not only did we see the advantages of them with the efficiency and easier way of life they will provided for us but also despite how they already exist today, Autonomous mobiles still have huge design problems such as the limitations on weather, legal laws and so on.

In the tutorial we took apart and defined the stages of the design process. The four main phases of the design project are discover – the planning and observing to understanding the scenario and problem to are designing to solve , define – exploration and research to define the core problems that needs to be addressed, develop – collaboration, prototyping and testing, Collaborating discover and define to come up concepts then working and testing the concepts on the stakeholders and deliver to develop a robust solution, deliver -refining and communicating the idea and design.We learnt how to do stakeholder mapping, which is an activity in the discovery phase to help identify and scope the problem area. We then did a target audience activity to narrow down the specific group of people

In the studio session as a group we came up with three individual ideas and areas. Tom wanted to look into delivery drones in the area of medical, Zhou looked into food deliver autonomous vehicles and I looked into the snow context and people needing help. With that we did our own stakeholder map and talk and refined each person’s ideas.

I looked into through secondary research what processes right now are used to find and help someone at the snow. WEEK 3

This week we identified the “User Research was a systematic approach to understanding people’s behaviours and needs to create useful, usable and desirable experiences.” We went into depth about the stages of the User research. We looked into what research questions and goals are and how they will be achieved by the multitude of different research methods and then using the information gathered by the methods and analysing it to summarize the found insights and conclusions.

In the studio, we broke down the components of the research plan in more depth. We came up with our own research questions for a design brief called Cooking Tech. After that we designed our own research plan and presented it for Cooking tech.

In the studio we changed from Sean’s group to Wills group and got told that instead of each person in our group to have three different contexts to research that we should all choose one of the three. We brainstormed various ideas of being in the context of the snow and problems spaces which could be improved by autonomous vehicles such as driving on icy roads, people getting lost in the mountains, handicapped people who are unable to ski without having people leading them and being injured at the top of the mountain. As a group, we decided to go with helping someone who is lost at the snow context because we also a lot of potential from secondary research in how methods can be improve with efficiency, costly and safety. We narrowed down the context looking into delivery drone and being lost and then came up with a multitude of research questions and then narrow down to five that we thought we should all answer in our research plan.

    1.How do most people become lost in the first place?
    2.What uses could the snow patrol have using a drone?
    3.How do people react when they are lost?
    4.What technology do people have with them when they are skiing/ how do people signal for help?
    5.How do people know what to do when they are lost?

After that we wanted each person to use different research methods in their research plans. After each research question we came up with a couple of research methods that would be the best fitting in answering the question. I used a questionnaire and an online survey.

Through the research method of emailing different Ski Patrol, emergency services and rescue squads long answered responses in what happens when someone is lost in a remote area. I found a lot of interesting results and saw a lot of potential in how using delivery, search drones could improve the experience of being lost at the snow. Found out information about how different circumstances results to different search parties and the procedures that the Ski patrol undergo before going on the search like contracting people who had last seen the lost people for general information about intentions, where they were last seen, state of mind, clothing etc. This gave me good insight in what the drone needs to find out first and send to the ski patrol. Also helped know what health risk were at hand when an individual was found in this situation and what should be in the first aid kit

The survey tested people who would be in a situation to be lost and how they would react being lost. The survey helped understand how many individuals don’t know first aid or what to do when lost. This came up with the idea of having a what to do when... page and a first aid instruction feature. We understood what people feel when they are lost and that all they want is someone there to comfort, so we tried to give features on the first aid kit to comfort and reduce their stress. We tried to come up with features for the first aid kit to make being lost as pleasant as possible.

Using the affinity diagram from the research material the three topics I wanted thought had to be achieved in the User Interface and drone were

    • Something familiar and reassuring can reduce panicking when lost
    • Procedures of what to do when being lost and in first aid needs to be more educated
    • Asking questions helps narrow how the person got lost and how can we gain this information from drone?

From the information I formed two storyboards and two personas about the situation now and what would happen.

We came up with 4 main ideas and broadened them you the different features they could have. They were the user interface for the ski patrol to find the person, the user interface for the people who is lost using a delivery, first aid drone, the autonomous snow mobile that comes and picks up the lost, injury people and takes them to safety of the drone that surveys the land for the ski patrol to have a safe route. I started to sketch and annotate using the technique shown in the lecture the storyboard. I found a series of aspect challenging. Secondly, it was challenging in how the drone will get a first kit to match their needs of the user or what will the drone do once it finds the person. I came up with the idea that the drones will receive a signal from the users lift pass when it hasn’t moved for a while or when it is out of the resort and will fly to the user. The drone will fly down and ask if the person needs assistance because the user might not want help and if they do then fly don’t and give the user the interface while it stays there until the ski patrol comes.

WEEK 8

In the lecture, we explored prototyping further. It was defined in the lecture as “a way of exploring, communicating and testing a vision of a future experience, by making it tangible.” We learnt about the different tools for creating high fidelity prototypes which help us understand what program is best for our prototype. For a successful prototype we learnt about the importance of animation, timing and motion, giving us ideas in how we can make the best possible prototype for ourselves.

In the tutorial, they taught us the tools of Invision and framer. In Invision they taught us how to use the application to create working and functioning prototype. We used the techniques learnt in the tutorial to create our own. The tutorial was extremely helpful and practical.

In the studio, we came together and consolidated all the feedback that we received in the user testing and brainstormed different features that should go into the User interface and different ways we could approach the design flaws from the research to improve the prototype as best as possible.

From the user feedback, we started to develop the high-fidelity wireframes. The colour blue was chosen because from research it has the effect to calm people down which was our goal. We wanted the interface to be clear and simple, so we added colourful icons that stand out from the grey and for people to get a deeper understanding of the features, limiting the confusion. We add more features into the different categories like movie, games, weather, meditation, call paramedics/ friends and a lot more. We put a search bar in the corner, so the user can access files sooner. From the user research we changed a lot of the layout such as got rid of the slider format in the stressed feature page and made it a grid because from the feedback it showed that the grid was a clearer, to the point layout. We struggled with thinking of ways in how we can simplify and categorise the medical page because the user in the low fidelity page felt that it was too overwhelming and unstructured with having topics of everybody function and issue so we decided that if the person just taps on the body where they are hurt and then answer a series of questions to narrow down the injury and then say how to help would be the most convent and helpful way. We kept the question section how it was but coloured the different answers. Overall the user research helped immensely to create a more straight forward and efficient user interface.

WEEK 10

In week 10’s lecture it gave us insight of the jobs opportunities in the field of design and how it is important to mix the skills of a designer and a programmer. This week the reports the due. I felt that as a group we struggled working together. In a lot of the lectures and studios at least one partner was away so when putting all our sketches and designs together everyone had done the task in a different way. The feedback received was useful with the icons being too colourful making the screen busy, the hierarchy of elements needed to be improved especially in a time of stress, there should be an option to call before answering the survey in desperate times and a more defined idea and process. All this feedback was really useful in the next stage of re-iterating the design.

WEEK 11

This week, in a group re-iterated the different things that had to be changed to create a successful design. We split up into tasks, Zhou was doing the Html of the prototype, I was re-design the different screens and Tom was doing the companion website and testing. To start I want to test different colour palettes to make the interface more appealing to the eye. I tested different things like when you hover over the screen the dull blue button with change colours but then realised that our interface is touch screen, so we couldn’t hover. I made all the icons white and added no background colour, but it made the buttons not look like they work. I came up with a few different designs and asked various people what they preferred. The preferred design was to keep the blue background and have all the icons white. We fiddled around with the hierarchy order putting things most important bigger and at the top of the pages. We decided from the studio and user research to delete the questions at the start and instead make a page asking if they want instant help to call the Ski patrol or self-assist where they can access the feature page. We gave shadows to the buttons to make them clearer. We changed a lot of headings like stress to stress relief, GPS to location of the ski patrol creating a greater understanding of the functions and buttons. We changed a lot of small features to create a more aesthetically pleasing design

WEEK 12

This week was when the assessments were due, we had a huge challenge that using HTML was a lot of hard work and was a great challenge with time restraints to be able to finish the reiterated high-fidelity prototype in time. Will told us that we didn’t need to use HTML and instead using Indesign studio application was a great and efficient way to put the prototype together and being able to add animations and create a better motion. Sadly, all that work on HTML was thrown out and we had to quickly re-design our sketch prototype to the way we want it and put it into design studio. We also added and iterated from the feedback we received from Will in more things that we should consider about the features of the design. From the feedback we put the stress section into medical section because it comes more under medical attention then having to be a section of its own. We deleted the section of recreational because sitting in the snow when lost watching a movie or reading a book is impractical and isn’t necessary for a rescue drone. With help from the tutor’s input, we decided that calling was one of the biggest essentials and should be on the home screen and it satisfies the goals of comforting someone by contacting help and reassurance. We also changed the heading format, deleted the headings icon and switched the side bar. The heading was taking too much of the screen up and wasn’t aesthetically pleasing to the eye, we made the heading smaller. We deleted the icon in the heading because it was clashing with the side bar and not making it noticed. We added animations like the breathing exercises, the call button changing colour, the side bar sliding in and the notification pop ups making the user interface flow. We gave motion to the slide as when the user presses the button the next page, slides right to make it look over the previous screen and if we wanted to go back the screen would slide left. Overall the user-face design was handed in on time and has been iterated to create a successful application. We went through a lot of challenges but in the end managed to make huge iterations to the user interface that is easy and efficient to comforts, give medical assistance and reduce stress to someone who is lost in the snow. I believe the interface we created is successful in achieving all our initial goals.
